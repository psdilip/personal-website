If you're starting a cloud journey, or just want to understand what all the fuss is about, AWS is a reasonable place to start — it's held the top spot in cloud market share for over a decade and is still one of the fastest-growing cloud businesses out there.

### What "the cloud" actually is

Think of it like electricity. You don't build your own power plant to run a house — you pay for what you use, when you use it. Cloud computing works the same way with compute, storage, and everything else that used to mean buying and racking your own hardware.

The old way meant weeks of planning hardware specs, ordering equipment, building in redundancy, and configuring all of it before you'd served a single request. AWS has already built globally distributed datacenters with high availability baked in — you can deploy something in minutes and pay only for what you actually consume.

That's a real edge for startups, and increasingly the reason larger companies migrate too: lower cost, and a much simpler way to manage applications, data, and permissions.

### What you can actually do with it

Databases, virtual servers, file systems, and 200+ other services covering everything from raw compute to machine learning. Some of the most common starting points:

- Hosting a website or server
- Building a full CI/CD pipeline
- Serverless functions with Lambda
- Cheap, durable file storage
- Email, SMS, and queueing services
- Fully managed versions of almost everything above

### Setting up the account

1. Go to the AWS sign-up page
2. Enter your email and choose a password
3. Continue to the next step
4. Fill in your personal details
5. Accept the terms and continue
6. Add billing information (free tier usage won't be charged)
7. Click **Verify and Continue**
8. Verify your identity with a phone number
9. Click **Continue**
10. Choose **Basic Support** if this is for personal projects
11. Finish the sign-up flow
12. Head to the AWS Management Console
13. Sign in as the **Root User**
14. Enter the email you signed up with
15. Complete the security check
16. Enter your password

### Where to go next

Once you're in, the best next step is just to start building something small — AWS's own tutorials are a good starting point, and a few reasonable first projects are a personal website, an automated SMS sender, or getting an IoT device talking to AWS.
