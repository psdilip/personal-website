There is a specific sound a South Indian filter coffee makes when you pour it between the tumbler and the dabarah — a small metallic waterfall — and it is the sound of my grandmother's kitchen at six in the morning.

You need decoction, strong and dark, and milk hot enough to matter. The pour is not for show. It cools the coffee and builds the froth in one motion.

It takes four minutes and it tastes like a whole childhood. Some recipes are worth keeping exactly as they were handed to you.
