title: The slow train through the Scottish Highlands
slug: slow-train

excerpt: Three days, no itinerary, and what happened when I stopped optimizing the trip and started noticing it.
date: 2026-06-20
---

The plan, if you could call it that, fit on the back of a receipt. Get to Glasgow. Buy a rail pass. Point myself north and see how far the mood carried me.

What I found first was that a train moving slowly through empty country does something strange to time. You look up and forty minutes have gone and you have no idea what you were thinking about, only that it was pleasant.

> Optimizing a trip and enjoying a trip turn out to be two different skills, and I had only ever practiced one of them.

By the third morning I had stopped checking the time entirely, which is where the good part of any trip actually begins.
