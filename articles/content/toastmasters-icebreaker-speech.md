Fellow Toastmasters — have you ever had your name used in all sorts of ways?

Mine's Sai Dilip Ponnaganti, and "Sai" alone has been through a lot — chemistry class turned it into "saianide," rhyming it with cyanide, and it's had a few other phonetic lives since. I've made peace with all of them.

**What I do:** I'm a cloud engineer — cloud computing infrastructure is the day job.

**What I do with the rest of my time:** I have a hard time saying no to things, so call me a Sai of all trades, master of none — writing, video games, tennis, the gym, flying drones, photography, cooking, and poking at whatever new piece of technology shows up. Starting a podcast has probably been the thing I'm proudest of — it's taught me that asking the right question, out of genuine curiosity, is most of the work.

**Where I am right now:** this feels like a meaningful stretch of life. Since the pandemic I moved back in with my parents, and that's deepened those relationships more than I expected. The last couple of years have mostly been about healing and self-discovery through therapy — prioritizing that over chasing the next professional milestone.

---

**Feedback I got:** clear delivery, strong projection, confident and relaxed, good humor, an attentive room, and vocal variation that's coming along. Suggestions — a bit more length, more hand gestures, some visual aids, and more direct eye contact with the audience.
